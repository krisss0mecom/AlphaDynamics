🧬 AlphaDynamics

Compact phase-oscillator neural propagator for protein torsion dynamics

2.39× lower JSD than Microsoft Timewarp · 9,652× fewer parameters

AlphaDynamics is a sequence-only neural propagator that predicts the equilibrium Ramachandran (φ, ψ) density of any peptide from sequence alone. Built on phase-oscillator dynamics rather than Cartesian transformer matrices.

Quickstart

pip install -U alphadynamics

# Predict torsion ensemble
alphadynamics predict --sequence KLVFFAE -o klvffae.npz

# Reconstruct 3D backbone (NEW v0.4.0)
alphadynamics rebuild klvffae.npz -s KLVFFAE -o klvffae.pdb --diagnostics

# Visualize
pymol klvffae.pdb     # animated 3D backbone trajectory

What it does

  • Predicts torsion dynamics — equilibrium ensemble of (φ, ψ) angles per residue from sequence alone
  • Reconstructs 3D backbone — deterministic NeRF (Parsons 2005) converts torsion to multi-model PDB
  • Diagnostics — per-frame radius of gyration and end-to-end distance
  • Cross-platform — runs on CPU (Jetson Orin Nano works), no GPU required

Architecture

Phase-oscillator neural ODE on the torus S¹×S¹: 160 coupled oscillators with RK4 integration, von Mises mixture output. ZERO nn.Linear, ZERO nn.Conv — pure phase dynamics. Inductive bias matched to natural geometry of torsion angles yields 30× data efficiency vs Cartesian transformers.
