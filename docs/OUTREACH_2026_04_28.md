## Technical Abstract For Communities

AlphaDynamics is a compact per-system neural propagator for protein backbone
torsion dynamics. The model maps current φ/ψ torsions to oscillator phases,
evolves them through a learned phase-flow ODE, and predicts the next-frame
torsion distribution with a mixture-of-von-Mises head. In a residue-index
aligned mdCATH audit at 348 K, AlphaDynamics beats a matched MLP baseline on
40/40 domains: 20/20 wins at N=48 with a 7.66x ratio of mean NLLs, and 20/20
wins at N=98 with a 5.08x ratio. Six 2500-step autoregressive rollouts produce
stable Ramachandran free-energy maps, with mean JSD 0.194 at N=48 and 0.172 at
N=98. The work is deliberately scoped as a per-system surrogate trained from
seed MD, not a zero-shot sequence-to-dynamics model.

## Best Next Scientific Upgrade

The strongest next result is not more promotion. It is a better baseline:

1. Add a stronger autoregressive baseline or simple normalizing-flow baseline in
   torsion space.
2. Run 3 random seeds on a smaller but representative subset.
3. Report confidence intervals.
4. Add one direct comparison to a public transferable model if technically
   feasible.

This would move the project from "interesting independent preprint" to
"harder-to-ignore benchmark result."
